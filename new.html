The Penan Weaving Process

From rainforest to refined luxury—witness the journey of authentic rattan craftsmanship

1

Sustainable Harvesting

Penan men sustainably harvest rattan from the Malaysian Borneo rainforest, following traditional "Molong" principles—never taking more than nature can replenish.

2

Careful Preparation

Rattan is stripped, cleaned, and split into fine strands. This meticulous preparation can take days, requiring deep knowledge of the material's properties.

3

Traditional Dyeing

Natural dyes create rich, earthy tones. Some artisans use plastic straps for vibrant colors, blending tradition with contemporary aesthetics.

4

Expert Weaving

Penan women weave intricate patterns passed down through generations. Each basket takes 2-7 days to complete, with every piece unique.

5

Quality Finishing

Final touches include reinforced handles, protective treatments, and quality inspection. The result: durable art that lasts for years.

6

Direct to You

We purchase directly from artisans at prices they set, ensuring fair compensation. Your hotel receives authentic craft with a documented story.

Meet the Makers

The Penan are one of the last nomadic tribes in Southeast Asia, with profound knowledge of the rainforest ecosystem. For generations, they've practiced the art of rattan weaving—creating everything from carrying baskets to sleeping mats.

Today, faced with deforestation and modernization, Penan women use their ancestral skills to earn sustainable income. Your partnership helps preserve this endangered cultural heritage while supporting entire families.

When you choose Penan craft, you're choosing:

  • Fair wages for indigenous artisans
  • Education opportunities for Penan children
  • Infrastructure improvements in remote villages
  • Preservation of traditional knowledge
